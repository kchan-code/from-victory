# From Victory — Brand Identity

> Rooted in the Word. Fueled by the Spirit. Built for Victory.

This document is the canonical brand reference for From Victory. All agents
read this when working on content, UI, copy, or visual identity. It is
derived from the comprehensive Logo + Brand Identity Design Document
prepared for creative refinement and vector execution.

## 1. Brand Mission

From Victory is a faith-based Christian mental toughness, daily discipline,
mindset training, and reflection app for athletes.

The brand must feel premium, athletic, gender-neutral, and modern while
remaining spiritually grounded. The product must not look or feel like a
generic ministry app, a youth group emblem, or a Bible-app clone. It is a
serious performance brand with biblical depth underneath.

## 2. Brand Positioning (CRITICAL — agents must internalize)

From Victory is first a **mental toughness training app with faith built in.**

It is NOT primarily a devotional app with sports language added.

| Layer | Role |
|---|---|
| Foundation | Secure identity in Christ |
| Method | Daily discipline |
| Outcomes | Mental toughness, confidence, resilience |
| Experience | Flexible modules that mature into progressive training plans |

Language reflects this ordering. The athlete enters a "daily training,"
not a "devotional." Scripture is the foundation that grounds the training,
not the wrapper around it.

## 3. Audience

- **Primary audience:** athletes ages 13-21 — middle school through
  college. A wide band. The frameworks hold across it; examples and
  register do not. Content calibrates per piece.
  - 13-15 (U15): younger, more concrete, simpler register
  - 16-18 (U18 / junior): recruiting, cuts, depth charts, identity pressure
  - 18-21 (junior / college; legal adults): autonomy, high stakes, owning
    their own faith
- **Buyer (MVP):** parents — evangelical / non-denominational Protestant
  lean. A future fork will let 18+ athletes self-onboard and self-pay.
- **Influencers:** coaches, team chaplains, hockey associations
- **Beachhead sport:** hockey. Brand must work across all sports long-term.

These are serious athletes — many training 10+ hours/week in scouted
leagues. Bullshit detectors are sharp across the whole band. Never "kid"
in athlete-facing content. Always "athlete," "player," or direct "you."

Audience language rules live in CLAUDE.md "Audience language" section.

## 4. Brand Personality

A blend of three energies:

- **Bold warrior** — disciplined, courageous, confident
- **Calm champion** — steady, grounded, secure
- **Modern performance app** — polished, premium, technically excellent

Never harsh. Never shame-based. Never overly aggressive.

## 5. Voice Modes

The content trio (content-curator + sports-psychologist + youth-pastor)
flexes between four voice modes depending on the moment in the experience.
content-curator owns the decision of which mode applies.

| Mode | When to use | Tone |
|---|---|---|
| Mentor | Default; most content | Steady, wise, encouraging |
| Coach | Action moments (pre-training, drills, ritual) | Direct, focused, disciplined |
| Devotional guide | Reflection moments (scripture, journal prompts) | Biblical, honest, formative |
| Teammate | Encouragement moments (return after a gap, celebration of effort) | Supportive, simple, no shame |

All four modes share the same underlying voice — only the register shifts.
The athlete should feel like the same trusted adult is speaking throughout,
adjusting how they speak based on what's needed.

## 6. Canonical Phrases

These exact phrases are the brand spine. Use them consistently.

### Public-facing
- **Tagline:** "Your Identity Is Secure. Compete From Victory."
- **Product positioning:** "Daily training for the mind, body, and spirit
  of the Christian athlete."

### Internal anchor (used in design and content briefings)
- "Rooted in the Word. Fueled by the Spirit. Built for Victory."

### Theological spine
- **Hebrews 12:1-2** — the foundational frame. "Let us run with
  perseverance the race marked out for us, fixing our eyes on Jesus,
  the pioneer and perfecter of faith. For the joy set before him he
  endured the cross." We run with endurance fixed on the One who
  already ran his race for us — competing FROM that completed work,
  not striving TOWARD a victory we have to earn.

## 7. Gamification Principle (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

**Reward participation and return. Never identity or spiritual worth.**

- No leaderboards
- No comparison to other athletes
- No public scoring
- No shame-based streak punishment
- An athlete who skips three days and returns sees encouragement, not
  "streak broken"
- Internal data can track current_streak / longest_streak — but the user-
  facing concept is **rhythm**, not streak. Visualize consistency without
  punishing breaks.

## 8. Logo System

Locked direction: **Open-book V + centered flame + cross-T wordmark.**

### Symbolism

- **The V** — victory. Wide, stable, athletic. Not tall, narrow, or
  medieval-sharp.
- **The Open Book** — the V doubles as an open book, pointing to the
  Word as foundation. Subtle, not literal. The icon must not read as a
  Bible-app icon.
- **The Flame** — Holy Spirit, inner fire, disciplined daily training.
  Premium, upward-moving, not cartoonish.
- **The Cross-T** — appears only in the wordmark, as a subtle cross
  inside the T of VICTORY. Never in the standalone icon. Reads as a T
  first, a cross second.

### Architecture

- **Primary stacked lockup** — icon above FROM / VICTORY. For splash,
  hero, onboarding.
- **Horizontal lockup** — icon left, wordmark right. For navigation,
  headers, decks.
- **Icon-only mark** — open-book V + centered flame. For app icon,
  social profile, stickers, apparel, favicon.
- **Wordmark-only** — FROM VICTORY with cross-T. For narrow layouts.

### Logo Rules

- No cross in the standalone icon. The cross belongs only in the wordmark T.
- No shields, crowns, athlete silhouettes, sport-specific imagery.
- No metallic 3D effects in the official logo. Flat only.
- No glow, outline, distortion, rotation, or stretch of the official mark.
- Maintain generous clear space around the mark.
- Create both detailed (with page-line details) and simplified small-size
  icon versions.

## 9. Color System

Signature treatment: warm athletic gold on near-black.

| Color | Role | Hex |
|---|---|---|
| Onyx Black | Primary background | #050505 |
| Deep Charcoal | Secondary background, cards | #101010 |
| Warm Athletic Gold | Primary logo, accent | #DFAF37 |
| Bright Gold Highlight | Digital highlight only | #F4C24F |
| Clean White | Wordmark, high-contrast text | #F7F7F7 |
| Silver White | Secondary wordmark, secondary text | #D9DCE1 |
| Electric Cobalt | UI progress / interactive accent only | #245BFF |
| Deep Royal Navy | Optional background depth | #071A33 |
| Deep Purple | Faith-forward depth option | #24113F |

### Color rules

- Logo signature: gold on black
- Logo secondary: white on black
- Logo light mode: black on white
- App icon: black/gold or gold/black
- Avoid low-contrast gold on light backgrounds
- Cobalt is NEVER in the logo. Reserve cobalt for UI progress, interaction
  states, and performance accents inside the app.

## 10. Typography

| Use case | Style | Guidance |
|---|---|---|
| Logo wordmark | Athletic premium sans | Strong uppercase, slightly forward, not esports-heavy |
| App headings | Modern geometric sans | Clean, confident, high contrast |
| Body / UI | Readable humanist or modern sans | Calm, accessible, comfortable for journaling and Scripture |
| Scripture callouts | Modern sans or restrained serif accent | Elevated but not decorative |

Until brand fonts are licensed, use Inter via next/font as a placeholder
across UI.

## 11. Visual Design Principles

- **Dark-mode-first.** Light mode is optional and secondary. Default to
  dark across all athlete-facing surfaces.
- **Premium, not flashy.** Generous spacing, considered typography,
  minimal ornamentation.
- **Performance brand language.** This is a training app. The visual
  language borrows from elite athletic brands, not from generic
  faith-based apps.
- **Mobile-first.** PWA installable to home screen. Design for 375px
  width before any wider breakpoint.

## 13. Words to Avoid (block in content review)

- Spiritual score
- Faith rank
- God points
- Worth score
- Better Christian level
- Streak broken
- Failure language tied to missed days
- "Pray harder and you'll [outcome]" (prosperity gospel)
- "God is on our side" (athletic triumphalism)

## 14. Product Design Implications

- Daily habit model: flexible modules first, progressive training plans later
- No shame-based gamification; rhythm over streak punishment
- Sport-adaptive: core brand is sport-neutral, hockey is the first content path
- Faith comfort level: can flex in explicitness across contexts, always
  grounded in Scripture
- Gamification rewards participation and return, never identity
