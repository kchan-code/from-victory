# Voice + guardrails — any copy written in this repo

The engine produces the marketing copy, but app UI strings, waitlist emails, store
listings, and microcopy get written here. They follow the same rules.

## The voice

- **Founder register: hockey dad, plain and observed.** Not poetic, not slogan. The
  canonical source is the founder's About copy on fromvictoryapp.com — mine it, don't
  improvise.
- **Verse-led.** One passage carries the spiritual weight; the application copy stays
  plain and concrete, tied to a real competing moment. Never manufactured profundity.
- **Concrete over conceptual** (KC, 2026-07-08). Show the behavior, don't state the
  theory. "Makes a mistake early and then plays the next ten shifts tight, careful,
  trying to get it back" — yes. "Competes from a settled identity or spends the next
  ten plays trying to earn one back" — no. If a line names a root cause most athletes
  wouldn't recognize in themselves, rewrite it as what it looks like.
- House style underneath: radical brevity; no AI words (delve, leverage-as-verb,
  utilize, comprehensive, crucial); no em-dashes; no emojis; action-forward.

## Hard guardrails

1. **Never pain-first.** Don't open on athlete failure or shame. It reads as an
   anxiety-fix wellness app.
2. **Never corny.** The always-say lines ("from victory, not for victory"; "your
   identity is secure") are real but must be earned in the copy, used once, never
   chanted.
3. **Never these words:** grind, streak, shame, earn-your-worth, or any
   performance-centered framing. The product's refusal to say them is part of the
   positioning.
4. **No prosperity-gospel "claim the win."** Worth is not the scoreboard — that's the
   thesis; don't break it.
5. **Pre-launch honesty.** No invented proof. Waitlist-only CTAs until launch.
6. **Claim visualization, don't knock it.** Everyone says visualize; almost nobody helps
   you do it; From Victory does — and you don't do it alone. (Don't dismiss
   visualization/mental training as hollow; that framing was rejected.)
7. **Closing note:** "Play hard, fearless, and free." is KC's approved closing line.
