1. Executive Summary





From Victory is a faith-based Christian athlete mindset and mental toughness app. The brand must feel premium, athletic, gender-neutral, and modern while remaining spiritually grounded. The logo should not look like a generic ministry logo, a youth group emblem, or a Bible app clone. It should feel like a serious performance brand with biblical depth underneath.





The locked logo direction is an open-book V with a centered rising flame, paired with a wordmark where the T in VICTORY becomes a subtle cross. This gives the app icon a clean, performance-oriented feel while allowing the full logo to reveal the Christ-centered foundation.





Core mark: Open-book V + centered flame.





Full wordmark: FROM VICTORY with the T as a subtle cross.





Core idea: Athletes compete from the victory already won in Christ, not to earn identity or salvation.





Internal design anchor: Rooted in the Word. Fueled by the Spirit. Built for Victory.





Public tagline: Your Identity Is Secure. Compete From Victory.





2. Brand Strategy Foundation





Audience





The initial audience is youth athletes, followed by college athletes and parents/coaches as buyers and supporters. Hockey is the beachhead because it is the founding context, but the brand must work across all sports.





Brand Personality





The brand should blend the energy of a bold warrior, the steadiness of a calm champion, and the polish of a modern performance app. It should be disciplined and confident without being harsh, shame-based, or overly aggressive.





Product Positioning





From Victory is first a mental toughness training app with faith built in. It is not primarily a devotional app with sports language added. Daily discipline is the method. Mental toughness, confidence, and resilience are the outcomes. Secure identity in Christ is the foundation.

3. Locked Logo Direction





The preferred direction is the Open-Book V + Centered Flame + Cross-T Wordmark. The icon should be strong enough to stand alone as an app icon, social avatar, hoodie mark, hat embroidery, helmet sticker, and workbook/journal cover symbol.









Primary preferred direction selected by the team.





Creative Direction Statement





Create a flat, premium logo for From Victory using a wide V that also subtly reads as an open book. Place a centered flame rising from the middle of the book/V form. Do not include a cross inside the icon. Instead, replace the T in VICTORY with a subtle cross in the full wordmark. The final system should feel athletic, app-ready, gender-neutral, and spiritually grounded.





4. Symbol System and Meaning





The V





The V represents victory. It should feel wide, stable, athletic, and confident. The V should not be tall, narrow, sharp in a medieval way, or overly aggressive.





The Open Book





The V also subtly suggests an open book, pointing to the Word of God as the foundation of the athlete’s identity, discipline, and mental toughness. The book meaning should be clear when explained, but not so literal that the app icon becomes a Bible-app icon.





The Flame





The flame represents the Holy Spirit, spiritual conviction, inner fire, focus, courage, and disciplined daily training. The flame should rise from the center of the open-book V, reinforcing the idea that the athlete’s growth flows from being rooted in the Word and fueled by the Spirit.





The Cross-T





The cross belongs in the wordmark, not the standalone icon. The T in VICTORY should become a subtle cross. It should read as a T first and a cross second. This keeps the app icon premium and subtle while allowing the full logo to be clearly Christ-centered.





5. Logo Architecture





Primary stacked lockup: Icon above FROM / VICTORY. Best for splash screens, hero sections, covers, and app onboarding.





Horizontal lockup: Icon left, wordmark right. Best for website navigation, app headers, decks, and email signatures.





Icon-only mark: Open-book V + centered flame. Best for app icon, social profile, stickers, apparel, and favicon.





Wordmark-only version: FROM VICTORY with the cross-T. Best for narrow layouts and secondary applications.

6. Visual Refinement Requirements





Icon Refinement





Preserve the open-book V shape from the locked direction.





Keep the flame centered and rising from the book/V intersection.





Use subtle page-line details in the primary mark, but create a simplified small-size version without relying on those lines.





Keep the official icon flat and clean.





Do not place the cross in the icon.





Flame Refinement





The flame should be smooth, premium, and not overly literal.





It should not feel like a campfire, torch, generic sports flame, or cartoon flame.





It should feel Spirit-fueled, disciplined, and upward-moving.





The flame should not be so tall that it dominates the V/book shape.





Wordmark Refinement





VICTORY should be dominant.





FROM should be smaller, spaced, and positioned above VICTORY in the stacked lockup.





The cross-T should be integrated into the typography and not feel pasted on.





Avoid esports-heavy, overly beveled, or overly aggressive letterforms.





The final wordmark should feel athletic, premium, and app-appropriate.

9. Usage Rules





Do





Keep the mark clean and scalable.





Use the icon by itself for app, social, favicon, apparel, and stickers.





Use the cross-T only in the full wordmark.





Create both detailed and small-size simplified icon versions.





Maintain generous clear space around the mark.





Use flat official logo files for production.





Don’t





Do not place a cross inside the standalone icon.





Do not add shields, crowns, athlete silhouettes, hockey sticks, pucks, helmets, or sport-specific imagery.





Do not make the icon overly churchy or generic ministry-like.





Do not use metallic 3D effects as the official logo.





Do not distort, stretch, rotate, outline, or add glow effects to the official mark.





Do not let page-line details become so thin that they disappear or clog at small sizes.





10. Brand Voice Guardrails

11. Product Design Implications





The logo and brand system should support a dark-mode-first app experience. The product should feel like a daily training environment for the mind and spirit, not a static devotional reader.





Daily habit model: Flexible modules first, later developing into progressive training plans.





No shame-based gamification: Use rhythm, not streak punishment.





Sport-adaptive structure: Core brand remains sport-neutral while hockey is the first content path.





Faith comfort level: The experience may flex in explicitness, but it should remain grounded in Scripture.





Gamification principle: Reward participation and return, never identity or spiritual worth.
