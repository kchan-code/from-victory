# Pregame Script Style — the de-corned voice (FV)

> **Status:** canonical voice spec for **pregame guided-audio scripts** (the
> visualization, hard-moment, and opener clips the athlete actually hears).
> Derived from KC's hand edits to the hockey + basketball scripts in
> `docs/pregame-scripts.md` (2026-06-15). Every agent that drafts or reviews
> pregame script prose reads this. It refines — does not replace — `docs/brand.md`.

## Why this exists

KC rewrote the hockey and basketball hard-moment and opener scripts to **strip
corniness, cheesiness, and cliché**. The old scripts leaned on a small set of
stock phrases that a sharp 13–21 athlete reads as fake — telegraphed body
checklists, mindfulness platitudes, and a repeated "it is not your identity /
reset and go again" tag glued onto the end of every cell. The voice below is
what replaced them: plainer, more specific, more like a real internal voice and
a real coach, and theologically grounded in the **opener** rather than
re-preached in every hard moment.

The brand spine is unchanged: **identity precedes performance; we compete FROM
victory.** What changed is *where* and *how* that truth is delivered — once, in
the opener's scripture, with weight; not as a tagline stapled to every reset.

## Part 1 — Hard-moment (HM) cells

A hard-moment cell rehearses one adversity (a penalty, a benching, nerves, a
missed chance) and walks the athlete through a reset. The de-corned shape:

```
1. Now rehearse the hard moment.                                    [0.4s]
2. <Scene — 2–4 short present-tense sentences, concrete and sport-true.> [1.5s]
3. <Specific observed body detail, 1–2 cues.> The thought hits: <intrusive self-talk>. [2s]
4. Now the reset. Return to your anchor.                            [3s]
5. <The reframe — one short, grounded truth. Sport-specific or a standard motif.> [2s]
6. Next <rep>, <2–3 concrete, sport-true actions>.                  [2s]
```

Most old cells were 7 lines; the de-corned form is usually **6**. Identity-heavy
cells (where the athlete's worth is the actual wound — see "the gated cells")
may keep a 7th `Speak the truth…` line. Routine tactical cells must not.

### The rules

**1. Kill the stock body checklist. Make line 3 specific and observed.**
The old line 3 was a template: `Feel what your body does. <Stomach drop / Heat
in your face / Tight chest / Shoulders up / Wind out of your lungs>. <bare
first-person thought>.` Every cell used the same three or four nouns. Replace
with **specific physical detail tied to this exact moment and this sport** —
what the eyes do, what the hands do, what the stick / glove / ball does, what
the feet do.

- ❌ `Feel what your body does. Heat in your face. Tight in your chest. I just put us down a man.`
- ✅ `Your stick feels too far from your body. Your feet are late. The thought hits: I gave them a power play.`

**2. Frame the negative self-talk as an intrusive thought: "The thought hits: ___".**
Name it as a thought passing through, not the athlete's settled belief. This is
cognitive defusion — the athlete learns to *notice* the thought instead of *being*
it. Use `The thought hits: ___` (default) or `You think, ___`. The quoted thought
may stay colloquial (`I'm not ready for this`, `I can't guard him`) — it's the
kid's inner voice. The narration around it stays plain and steady.

**3. Cut the mindfulness platitude.** The line `Come back to your breath. Come
back to right now.` (and `Come back to your base.`) is **banned**. It's
redundant with `Return to your anchor` and reads as canned. Go straight from the
anchor line to the reframe.

**4. Reset pause is `[3s]`.** `Now the reset. Return to your anchor.` is followed
by a 3-second silence (was 2s). The athlete needs the beat.

**5. End on a concrete next-rep cue, not boilerplate.** The old closer —
`Speak the truth. … It is not your identity. Reset and go again.` — is **cut from
routine cells**. The identity theology lives in the opener. The HM ends by
pointing the athlete at the very next rep with specific, coachable actions.

- ❌ `Speak the truth. That turnover is over. It is not your identity. Reset and go again.`
- ✅ `Next breakout, shoulder-check first, choose the simple play, and move it strong.`

**6. Reframe (line 5) is short and true, never preachy.** One or two sentences.
Often a standardized motif (below). It tells the truth about the moment without
sermonizing.

### Standardized reframes (reuse these — they're the de-corned motifs KC converged on)

| Adversity | Reframe line (line 5) |
|---|---|
| Coach yells / correction | `The volume is not the verdict. Take the correction. Leave the shame.` |
| Benched / pulled | `The bench has your body for a shift. It does not have your mind.` (+ next line: `Stay in it. The next chance may come fast.`) |
| Nervous | `These nerves are energy, not danger. Let them sharpen you.` |
| Beaten / cooked / got-by | `He won that <rush/switch/rep>. He does not own the next one.` |
| Fall behind early | `That run is over. Get one stop, then get one score.` (sport-analog: one out / one shot) |
| Penalty / whistle | `The whistle happened. Learn from it, then compete clean.` |

Adapt the vocabulary to the sport (a golfer doesn't "get one stop"), but keep the
shape: *name it over → deny it the next rep → point forward.*

### The gated cells (identity-collapse — handle with extra care)

A few adversities cut at the athlete's *worth*, not just the play: the yips-class
loss of a basic skill (`lose-command`, `first-tee`), the core-skill miss for a
player whose identity rides on it (the Scrambler's duffed chip, "if that's gone,
what do I have?"). These are **clinically gated** (withheld from the picker until
sign-off) and they're the one place the identity truth belongs *inside* the HM.
Here you may keep a fuller `Speak the truth…` line that says the worth was never
on the line — but still plainly, never as a slogan. Never name "the yips" or "the
shank." Keep the FV-119 gate flags (`⚠`) intact.

## Part 2 — Openers (the scripture clips)

The opener is where the scripture and the identity truth land. One verse,
delivered with weight, then applied to the game. The de-corned opener shape:

```
1. Close your eyes. <one settling cue>.                            [1s]
2. Hear this from <Book chapter:verse>. <one line of context>: <the quote, NIV>. [1s]
3. <Plain, warm exposition — what the passage actually says.>      [1s]
4. <The turn — what it means for this athlete, now.>               [1s]
5. <Identity / freedom line — secure before the game starts.>      [1s]
6. <Concrete in-game application: real calls, real actions.>       [1s]
```

### The rules

**1. Introduce the verse cleanly.** `Listen to what Paul wrote in Romans 8.` →
`Hear this from Romans 8:1.` Add the verse numbers. Keep the one-line human
context (`Paul wrote it from prison, not comfort`) — it earns the verse — but
drop the wordy wind-up.

**2. Drop the "[quote phrase] does not mean X" tic.** The old openers explained
every verse by negation (`Soar on wings like eagles does not mean you will never
feel tired. It does not mean prayer is a performance hack.`). It reads heady and
slightly defensive. Say what the passage *does* mean, warmly and plainly:
`Isaiah does not pretend you will never get tired. He says even the young grow
weary.`

**3. End on the game, not an abstraction.** The old openers closed on a mentor
aphorism (`Hope is not believing in yourself.`). The de-corned openers close on
**real in-game application** — actual calls, actual actions: `Head up. Hands
loose. Read the ice. Make the next faithful play.`

**4. Plain over clever.** Cut the edgy mentor abstractions: `the part of you that
has to look good`, `white-knuckling the game`, `release the death grip on
outcome`. They draw attention to the writer. Say it straight.

**5. NIV, cite chapter and verse, stay accessible across Protestant traditions.**
(Unchanged from `docs/brand.md`.)

opener clips (`opener-bb-*`) for sport-specific application language; baseball,
golf, and the v2 sports currently inherit the shared openers. When a verse
changes for one sport, it changes for all.

## Quick banned-phrase list

Do not write these into pregame scripts:

- `Feel what your body does.` (as a stock line-3 opener)
- `Come back to your breath. Come back to right now.` / `Come back to your base.`
- `It is not your identity.` / `It is not who you are.` / `Reset and go again.`
  (as a stock HM closer — the identity beat lives in the opener)
- `Listen to what <Paul> wrote in <Book>.` (use `Hear this from <Book ch:v>.`)
- `<Quoted phrase> does not mean…` (the teaching-by-negation tic)
- Bare italicized first-person thoughts (use `The thought hits: ___`)
- The stock somatic trio `Stomach drop. Heat in your face. Tight chest.` repeated
  cell to cell

## What did NOT change

- The **visualization (VIZ) positive-play** scripts were left as-is. The
  de-cheesing pass was HM cells + openers.
- The brand spine, the four voice modes, NIV, the age calibration, and the
  clinical gates all stand.
