# Pregame Script Style — the de-corned voice (FV)

> **Status:** canonical voice spec for **pregame guided-audio scripts** (the
> visualization, hard-moment, and opener clips the athlete actually hears).
> Derived from KC's hand edits to the hockey + basketball scripts (2026-06-15)
> and reconciled with the FV-339 four-beat reframe canon on **2026-07-08
> (FV-398)** — that revision supersedes any reading of this spec and the agents'
> FV-339 sections as contradictory (see "How this relates to the FV-339
> four-beat canon" below). Every rule and example in this spec has been verified
> against the shipped script books (`docs/scripts/hockey.md`, `basketball.md`,
> `golf.md`; baseball spot-checked). **When this spec and a KC-authorized book
> conflict, the book wins** — flag the discrepancy (see "Open KC decisions"),
> never silently edit either. Every agent that drafts or reviews pregame script
> prose reads this. It refines — does not replace — `docs/brand.md`.

## Why this exists

KC rewrote the hockey and basketball hard-moment and opener scripts to **strip
corniness, cheesiness, and cliché**. The old scripts leaned on a small set of
stock phrases that a sharp 13–21 athlete reads as fake — telegraphed body
checklists, mindfulness platitudes, and a repeated "it is not your identity /
reset and go again" tag glued onto the end of every cell. The voice below is
what replaced them: plainer, more specific, more like a real internal voice and
a real coach, and theologically grounded in the **opener** rather than
re-preached in every hard moment.

The brand spine is unchanged: **identity precedes performance; we compete FROM
victory.** What changed is *where* and *how* that truth is delivered — once, in
the opener's scripture, with weight; not as a tagline stapled to every reset.

The 2026-07-08 golden-set replay eval confirmed why this spec must be explicit:
6 of 7 blind curator drafts stapled the worth beat and the brand tagline into
routine hard-moment cells, in near-identical wording — the exact pre-June
failure mode. The specialists were clean; the corn entered at the integration
layer, where the FV-339 canonical blockquote was read as scriptable lines.

## How this relates to the FV-339 four-beat canon

The content agents (`content-curator`, `sports-psychologist`, `youth-pastor`)
carry an FV-339 section built around this canonical blockquote:

> These nerves are energy, not danger.
> It is not your identity.
> Your worth is secure.
> Reset and go again.

This spec's banned-phrase list bans two of those exact lines as stock HM
closers. **That is not a contradiction. The blockquote is a theological
summary of four TRUTHS; this spec governs the WORDS.** How KC actually
resolved it, per the shipped books:

- **The four truths are the canon SHAPE, not a slogan.** Every hard-moment
  reset embodies (1) arousal reappraisal, (2) identity ≠ performance,
  (3) secured worth, (4) a concrete reset cue. No cell recites them.
- **Beats 2–3 (identity / worth) are delivered once, with weight, in the
  OPENER** — the scripture clip that starts every session (e.g. the Reset
  opener on Romans 8:1: "The mistake you make tonight cannot reopen a case God
  has already closed."). Every hard moment the athlete rehearses happens
  *after* that truth has landed. The HM cell does not need to re-preach it;
  it inherits it.
- **Inside HM cells, beats 2–3 surface only where the wound is identity
  itself:** the clinically gated identity-collapse cells (see "The gated
  cells"), and — at most — a single plain worth *clause* woven into line 5 of
  an identity-heavy live cell (shipped example, `hm-goalie-pulled`: "The bench
  has your body for now. It does not have your worth."). One clause, plain,
  earned. Never the blockquote's wording, never a tagline.
- **Routine HM cells deliver beats 2–3 implicitly** — by treating the mistake
  as finished and refusing it a verdict ("That turnover is over." / "The
  volume is not the verdict.") — and spend their words on beats 1 and 4:
  the body, the intrusive thought named as a thought, a short true reframe,
  a concrete next-rep cue.
- **Writing the blockquote's beat-2/3/4 wording verbatim into a routine HM
  cell is a banned-phrase violation**, exactly as if you had written any other
  banned line. "Compete/skate/play from victory" as an HM closer is the same
  violation — the brand tagline belongs on marketing surfaces and in opener
  theology, not stapled to a reset.

If you are drafting and the four-beat section and this spec seem to pull in
different directions, this spec (and above it, the books) wins on wording;
the four-beat section wins on what must be *true* in the reset. They were
reconciled deliberately (FV-398); do not re-detect a conflict.

## Part 1 — Hard-moment (HM) cells

A hard-moment cell rehearses one adversity (a penalty, a benching, nerves, a
missed chance) and walks the athlete through a reset. The de-corned shape:

```
1. Now rehearse the hard moment.                                    [0.4s]
2. <Scene — 2–4 short present-tense sentences, concrete and sport-true.> [1.5s]
3. <Specific observed body detail, 1–2 cues.> The thought hits: <intrusive self-talk>. [2s]
4. Now the reset. Return to your anchor.                            [reset pause — see rule 4]
5. <The reframe — one short, grounded truth. Sport-specific or a standard motif.> [2s]
6. Next <rep>, <2–3 concrete, sport-true actions>.                  [2s]
```

Most old cells were 7 lines; the de-corned form is **6**. Only the clinically
gated identity-collapse cells (see "the gated cells") may carry a 7th
worth-truth line. Routine cells must not.

### The rules

**1. Kill the stock body checklist. Make line 3 specific and observed.**
The old line 3 was a template: `Feel what your body does. <Stomach drop / Heat
in your face / Tight chest / Shoulders up / Wind out of your lungs>. <bare
first-person thought>.` Every cell used the same three or four nouns. Replace
with **specific physical detail tied to this exact moment and this sport** —
what the eyes do, what the hands do, what the stick / glove / ball does, what
the feet do.

- ❌ `Feel what your body does. Heat in your face. Tight in your chest. I just put us down a man.`
- ✅ `Your stick feels too far from your body. Your feet are late. The thought hits: I gave them a power play.`
  (verbatim, `hm-forward-bad-penalty`, hockey book)

**2. Frame the negative self-talk as an intrusive thought: "The thought hits: ___".**
Name it as a thought passing through, not the athlete's settled belief. This is
cognitive defusion — the athlete learns to *notice* the thought instead of *being*
it. Use `The thought hits: ___` (default) or `You think, ___`. The quoted thought
may stay colloquial (`I forced that`, `I'm forcing things`) — it's the
athlete's inner voice. The narration around it stays plain and steady.

**2a. The thought's CONTENT is constrained — defuse, don't install (KC,
2026-07-09, FV-412).** A rehearsal script that voices a composed negative label
can INSTALL a story the athlete never had, rather than defusing one they have.
(The ruling case: the rendered baseball clip said `The thought hits: I lost the
staff. They don't trust me back there.` — KC ruled it dangerous.) A line-3
intrusive thought may be:

- **A behavioral read of the play/pattern** — KC's verbatim allowed examples:
  `I forced that` ✓ · `I'm forcing things` ✓ · `I'm playing outside of myself,
  doing too much` ✓
- **A generic naming of the mental noise** (for fear-of-recurrence moments):
  `self-doubt runs through my head` ✓ — name the doubt's arrival, never script
  its content.

It may NOT be:

- **A capability/identity verdict:** `I'm not ready for this` ✗ (KC-explicit —
  this line was previously cited in the authorized hockey forward-nervous cell
  AND in this rule's own pre-FV-412 examples; the FV-412 rule supersedes both)
- **A status/permanence verdict:** `I lost my spot` ✗ · `I'm done here` ✗ ·
  `I lost the staff` ✗
- **An other-party judgment read:** `he/they don't trust me` ✗ · `the pitcher
  can't trust me` ✗ · `coach thinks I can't play` ✗

(Exception path: the clinically gated identity-collapse cells, where the
identity lie is the clinical subject — see "Open KC decisions" item 8; those
route to FV-119, not to a mechanical application of this rule.)

**3. Cut the mindfulness platitude.** The line `Come back to your breath. Come
back to right now.` (and `Come back to your base.`) is **banned in HM cells**.
It's redundant with `Return to your anchor` and reads as canned. Go straight
from the anchor line to the reframe.

**4. Reset pause is a real hold — match your book.** `Now the reset. Return to
your anchor.` is followed by a silence long enough to actually reset in.
Shipped values: **`[3s]` in hockey and basketball** (KC lengthened it from 2s
in the 2026-06-15 pass); **`[2s]` uniformly in golf and baseball** (both
authored after the pass — all 30 golf and all 39 baseball cells). New cells in
an existing book match that book's shipped value. The default for future sport
books is an open KC decision (see "Open KC decisions").

**5. End on a concrete next-rep cue, not boilerplate.** The old closer —
`Speak the truth. … It is not your identity. Reset and go again.` — is **cut from
routine cells**. The identity theology lives in the opener. The HM ends by
pointing the athlete at the very next rep with specific, coachable actions.
The brand tagline (`from victory`, `compete from victory`) is equally banned
as an HM closer.

- ❌ `Speak the truth. That turnover is over. It is not your identity. Reset and go again.`
- ❌ `Play the next hole from victory.`
- ✅ `Next breakout, shoulder-check first, choose the simple play, and move it strong.`
  (verbatim, `hm-defense-turnover`, hockey book)

**6. Reframe (line 5) is short and true, never preachy.** One or two *short*
sentences — the shipped books run tighter here than drafts tend to (the eval's
failed drafts averaged 2–4 sentences where the gold runs 1–2). Often a
standardized motif (below). It tells the truth about the moment without
sermonizing.

### Standardized reframes (reuse these — they're the de-corned motifs KC converged on)

All quotes verbatim from the shipped books.

| Adversity | Reframe line (line 5) | Source |
|---|---|---|
| Coach yells / correction | `The volume is not the verdict. Take the correction. Leave the shame.` | hockey `hm-defense-coach-yells`; basketball `hm-bb-big-coach-yells` |
| Benched (hockey) | `The bench has your body for a stretch. It does not have your mind.` (Forward; Defense says `for a shift`) | hockey `hm-forward-benched` / `hm-defense-benched` |
| Benched (basketball) | `Stay in it. The next chance may come fast.` | basketball `hm-bb-guard-benched` / `hm-bb-wing-benched` |
| Pulled (identity-heavier) | `The bench has your body for now. It does not have your worth.` — the one live in-cell worth clause | hockey `hm-goalie-pulled` |
| Nervous | `These nerves are energy, not danger. Let them sharpen you.` | hockey / basketball / golf, multiple cells |
| Beaten / cooked / got-by | `He won that rush. He does not own the next one.` (golf: `He won that hole. He does not own the next one.`) | hockey `hm-defense-beaten-wide`; golf `hm-glf-bomber-outplayed` |
| Fall behind early | `That run is over. Get one stop, then get one score.` (Big: `…one stop, one board, then one score.`; golf: `That run is over. The deficit doesn't come back on one hero swing. Get one good shot, then get the next one.`) | basketball guard/wing/big `fall-behind-early`; golf `hm-glf-ballstriker-fall-behind` |
| Penalty / whistle | `The whistle happened. Learn from it, then compete clean.` | hockey `hm-forward-bad-penalty` / `hm-defense-bad-penalty` |

Adapt the vocabulary to the sport (a golfer doesn't "get one stop"), but keep the
shape: *name it over → deny it the next rep → point forward.*

### The gated cells (identity-collapse — handle with extra care)

A few adversities cut at the athlete's *worth*, not just the play: the
yips-class loss of a basic skill. These are **clinically gated**: the clip is
authored in the book, but the adversity is **withheld from the Step-02 picker**
in `apps/web/components/pregame/sport-registry.ts` (`roleAdversities`
overrides, FV-119 pattern) until clinical-advisor sign-off. Currently withheld:

- **golf `first-tee`** (all three profiles) — the swing-deserts-you umbrella
- **baseball `lose-command`** (Catcher + Infield — the throwing yips;
  Outfield's version is a bad throw, not gated)

This is the one place the worth truth belongs *inside* the HM, and the books
show two authorized registers for it:

- **A 7th worth-truth line, plain and earned** — baseball
  `hm-bsb-catcher-lose-command` line 7: `Your worth was secure before this
  throw and it's secure after it, so you can throw free.`
- **Identity woven into line 5, no extra line** — golf
  `hm-glf-ballstriker-first-tee` line 5: `This is happening to your hands right
  now, on this tee. It is nerves on the first tee, nothing more — not a
  verdict, not the swing you own.`

Either way: never as a slogan, never the FV-339 blockquote verbatim. Never name
"the yips" or "the shank" (verified: no book speaks either word). Note that
identity-*adjacent* cells are not automatically gated — golf's Scrambler
`duff-chip` (the core-skill miss, "one I make in my sleep") ships live and
selectable, and carries its worth truth implicitly: `That chip is over — one
swing today, not the thousands you've holed.` Keep the picker withholds intact;
re-enabling a gated cell is a KC + clinical decision, not an editorial one.

## Part 2 — Openers (the scripture clips)

The opener is where the scripture and the identity truth land. One verse,
delivered with weight, then applied to the game. The de-corned opener shape:

```
1. Close your eyes. <one settling cue>.                            [1s]
2. Hear this from <Book chapter:verse>. <one line of context>: <the quote, NIV>. [1s]
3. <Plain, warm exposition — what the passage actually says.>      [1s]
4. <The turn — what it means for this athlete, now.>               [1s]
5. <Identity / freedom line — secure before the game starts.>      [1s]
6. <Concrete in-game application: real calls, real actions.>       [1s]
```

### The rules

**1. Introduce the verse cleanly.** `Listen to what Paul wrote in Romans 8.` →
`Hear this from Romans 8:1.` Add the verse numbers. Keep the one-line human
context (`Paul wrote it from prison, not comfort` — verbatim, hockey Calm
opener) — it earns the verse — but drop the wordy wind-up.

**2. One honest negation is canon; the stacked-negation tic is not.** The old
openers explained every verse by strings of negation (`Soar on wings like
eagles does not mean you will never feel tired. It does not mean prayer is a
performance hack.`) — heady and slightly defensive. The shipped openers keep
**at most one** `does not mean X` clause, and always pivot immediately to what
the passage *does* mean:

- ✅ `No condemnation does not mean no consequences. If you turn it over, the
  puck still goes the other way. But it means the verdict on you was already
  settled at the cross.` (verbatim, hockey Reset opener)
- ✅ `Isaiah does not pretend you will never get tired. He says even the young
  grow weary. Even strong people stumble.` (verbatim, hockey Hope opener)

What's banned is negation with no affirmation pivot, or more than one negation
in a row.

**3. End on the game, not an abstraction.** The old openers closed on a mentor
aphorism (`Hope is not believing in yourself.`). The de-corned openers close on
**real in-game application** — actual calls, actual actions: `Head up. Hands
loose. Read the ice. Make the next faithful play.` (verbatim, hockey Decisions
opener). Do not echo the HM-closer shape here either — the opener's last line
is application, not a reset formula.

**4. Plain over clever.** Cut the edgy mentor abstractions: `the part of you that
has to look good`, `white-knuckling the game`, `release the death grip on
outcome`. They draw attention to the writer. Say it straight. (Concrete
physical description is fine — golf's `Your grip is white-knuckle tight` is an
observed body detail, not a mentor abstraction.)

**5. NIV, cite chapter and verse, stay accessible across Protestant traditions.**
(Unchanged from `docs/brand.md`.)

## Quick banned-phrase list

Confirmed against the live books 2026-07-08 (FV-398). Do not write these into
pregame scripts:

- `Feel what your body does.` (as a stock line-3 opener)
- `Come back to your breath. Come back to right now.` / `Come back to your base.`
  (and variants) in HM cells
- `It is not your identity.` / `It is not who you are.` / `Reset and go again.`
  / `Your worth is secure.` verbatim, and the tagline (`from victory`,
  `compete/skate/play from victory`) — as stock HM closers. The identity/worth
  beat lives in the opener and the gated cells (see the FV-339 section above).
- `Listen to what <Paul> wrote in <Book>.` (use `Hear this from <Book ch:v>.`
  — zero live occurrences remain)
- Stacked or unresolved negation in exposition (`does not mean X. It does not
  mean Y.`) — one negation with an immediate affirmation pivot is authorized
  (see Part 2, rule 2)
- Bare first-person thoughts (use `The thought hits: ___` or `You think, ___`)
- The stock somatic trio `Stomach drop. Heat in your face. Tight chest.` repeated
  cell to cell
- **Line-3-scoped (intrusive-thought CONTENT — FV-412, added 2026-07-09, not
  part of the FV-398 live-book confirmation):** banned as the quoted thought in
  line 3 — `not ready for this` · `don't trust me` / `doesn't trust me` /
  `can't trust me` · `can't be trusted` · `I'm done here` · `I lost my spot` ·
  `I lost the staff`. These are the greppable instances of the three banned
  classes (capability/identity verdict, status/permanence verdict, other-party
  judgment read) — see Part 1, rule 2a. Gated identity-collapse cells are
  exempted pending FV-119 (see "Open KC decisions" item 8).

A handful of live, KC-authorized cells still contain banned lines — they are
canon drift, catalogued under "Open KC decisions" below. Do not copy their
banned lines as precedent, and do not edit them without KC.

## What did NOT change

- The **visualization (VIZ) positive-play** scripts were left as-is. The
  de-cheesing pass was HM cells + openers.
- The **Full-Session Cells** sections of the hockey and basketball books are
  **legacy — not used in the compositional path**. They still contain the old
  7-line form (`Feel what your body does…`, `Speak the truth… It is not your
  identity. Reset and go again.`) throughout. They are not canon for new
  writing and are not catalogued as drift.
- The **text-mode fallback** lines (the on-screen no-audio path at the top of
  each book) predate the pass and still carry `Your mistake is real. It is not
  your identity.` — a different surface from the audio scripts this spec
  governs (flagged as a low-priority KC decision below).
- The brand spine, the four voice modes, NIV, the age calibration, and the
  clinical gates all stand.

## Open KC decisions (canon drift found in the 2026-07-08 sweep)

Every live (non-legacy) cell in the hockey, basketball, and golf books was
swept against the banned list; baseball was spot-swept (clean — zero banned
hits). The cells below are **KC-authorized and shipped**; each carries a
banned line or a rule-level inconsistency. For each, the options are
**(a) ratify as authorized** (this spec then records it as a named exception)
or **(b) re-cut the line and re-render** (audio regen + `MANIFEST_VERSION`
bump per FV-142). **Flag only — never edit the books without KC.**

1. **hockey `hm-forward-coach-yells`** — line 3: `Feel what your body does.
   Tight jaw. Heat in your chest. He is going to bury me.` — banned stock
   opener + bare first-person thought (no `The thought hits:`). Reset pause
   also still `[2s]`. (The finding that motivated FV-398.) *FV-412 note: the
   thought content itself (`He is going to bury me` — an other-party judgment
   read) also violates rule 2a; quoted here as shipped canon, superseded
   pending re-cut per FV-412.*
2. **hockey `hm-forward-start-slow`** — line 3: `Feel what your body does.
   Tight grip on the stick. Shoulders up. I am not in this game.` — banned
   stock opener + bare thought; **plus** a 7th line `Speak the truth. You do
   not need to chase the game…` on a routine (non-gated) cell; reset pause
   `[2s]`. The most complete miss of the de-corn pass.
3. **hockey `hm-defense-bad-penalty`** — line 3: `Feel what your body does.
   Heat in your face. Tight in your chest. You think, I just put us down a
   man.` — banned stock opener (the thought itself is correctly framed with
   `You think,`).
4. **basketball `hm-bb-guard-nervous`** — line 3: `Feel what your body does.
   Heart pounding in your ears. Fingers buzzing on the ball. What if I cough
   it up first possession.` — banned stock opener + bare thought; **plus**
   line 5: `Come back to your breath. Come back to the ball.` — the banned
   mindfulness platitude; reset pause `[2s]`.
5. **hockey/shared `opener-calm`** — line 6: `Come back to your breath. Come
   back to your next play. One read. One puck. One shift.` — a banned-list
   variant, though in the Calm opener (Philippians 4) where breath is
   thematically native and the line closes on the game. Plausibly intentional;
   ratify or re-cut.
6. **Reset-pause split (rule-level).** Hockey and basketball ship `[3s]` after
   the anchor line (except the drifted cells in items 1, 2, 4, which kept
   `[2s]`); golf ships `[2s]` in all 30 cells and baseball `[2s]` in all 39.
   Decide: is `[2s]` the ratified parameter for golf/baseball (and the default
   for future books), or should those books be re-rendered to `[3s]`?
7. **Text-mode fallback line (all books, low priority).** The on-screen
   fallback's adversity line still reads `…Your mistake is real. It is not
   your identity.` — outside the audio de-corn scope but visible to athletes
   in text mode. Align with the de-corned register, or ratify as a distinct
   surface.
8. **Gated identity cells' thought lines vs the FV-412 intrusive-thought rule
   (added 2026-07-09, FV-412).** The clinically gated identity-collapse cells
   carry thought lines whose content IS an identity verdict — golf `first-tee`
   (`if I can't trust the swing, I have nothing`) and the yips-class
   `lose-command` cells. There the identity lie is the clinical subject of the
   cell, not incidental script content. Route these to the FV-119 clinical
   review; do NOT mechanically apply rule 2a / the line-3-scoped banned classes
   to them. Any re-cut of a gated cell's thought line is a KC + clinical
   decision.
